# Clinivise

At Clinivise, my work sat at the intersection of healthcare data, analytics, automation, and internal platform development.

What made this interesting to me was the nature of the problem space itself. Healthcare operations are full of friction. There are data challenges, workflow bottlenecks, operational complexity, and a lot of room for better systems. That kind of environment is exactly where I like working, because meaningful engineering can create very practical value.

My role involved contributing to products and workflows related to analytics, workflow tooling, browser automation, and operational systems. Instead of treating those as separate concerns, I think of them as parts of one larger system: how do you reduce friction in a complex real-world process?

Clinivise also reflects a broader pattern in the kind of work I’m drawn to. I’m interested in domains where software is not just about features, but about making difficult operations simpler, more visible, and more manageable. That’s why healthcare automation and analytics became such a natural fit for me.

More broadly, Clinivise strengthened my interest in using engineering and AI methods in real-world domains where data, operations, and workflow design all matter together.
