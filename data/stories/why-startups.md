# Why Startups

I’m drawn to startup environments because they let me work close to the real problem.

What I enjoy most is not being boxed into one narrow layer of the system. I like understanding the user problem, shaping the product direction, figuring out the technical path, and then building the thing end to end. Startups usually create the kind of environment where that is possible.

I also like ambiguity when it is tied to momentum. A lot of 0→1 work involves unclear constraints, evolving priorities, and the need to make reasonable decisions without having every answer upfront. That style of building suits me well.

Another reason I like startups is that the feedback loop is closer. You can feel whether what you built matters. You can see whether a workflow got simpler, whether the product became more useful, or whether a system actually solved something painful.

For me, the appeal of startups is not just speed for the sake of speed. It’s the chance to build meaningful systems with strong ownership, practical impact, and room to connect engineering with product thinking.
