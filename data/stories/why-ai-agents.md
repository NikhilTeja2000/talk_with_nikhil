# Why AI Agents

I’m interested in AI agents because they make software feel closer to action.

A lot of software traditionally stops at displaying information or helping users navigate interfaces. AI agents create the possibility of systems that can understand context, reason across steps, assist more dynamically, and reduce friction in a more active way.

What draws me to this space is not just the novelty of AI. It’s the product possibility. I like systems that do something meaningful with complexity. When an agent is designed well, it can help connect information, workflow, interface, and action in a much more natural way than traditional software.

This fits well with the kind of builder I am. I’m drawn to automation, real-time systems, browser workflows, and products that feel alive rather than static. AI agents sit right at that intersection.

I also think AI agents are most interesting when they are grounded in real needs. I’m less interested in vague demos and more interested in practical systems that help people solve actual problems, especially in areas like operations, trust, workflow automation, and intelligent user experiences.
