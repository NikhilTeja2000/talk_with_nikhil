# About Me

I’m Nikhil Teja Chilakabattina, a founding engineer, full-stack engineer, and AI product builder.

A lot of what drives me is the idea of taking something complex, messy, or high-friction and turning it into a system that feels clear, usable, and alive. Over time, that mindset has shaped how I build products, how I think about engineering, and what kinds of problems I naturally move toward.

My background started with software engineering foundations in India, where I built an early interest in structured problem-solving and systems thinking. Later, during my time at VIT and then the University of Cincinnati, my interests expanded from pure development into product engineering, automation, AI, and more practical forms of intelligent software.

Professionally, I’ve worked across enterprise software, institutional engineering, startup-like environments, and AI-focused product work. At SOTI, I built a foundation in full-stack product execution. At the University of Cincinnati and related roles, I gained more experience building structured internal systems and practical software. Over time, I moved closer to AI-native product thinking, automation, and systems that help people deal with complexity more effectively.

What I enjoy most is not just writing code in isolation. I like building systems that connect product goals, technical depth, and real user value. That’s why I’m drawn to healthcare workflows, analytics platforms, browser automation, AI agents, and trust-oriented systems. These spaces are full of messy operational realities, and that makes them interesting to me.

I also care a lot about digital experiences that feel more alive than static. That includes conversational interfaces, real-time systems, AI agents, and products that are not just functional but expressive and adaptive.

If I had to summarize my direction in one line, it would be this:

I like building practical 0→1 systems that combine engineering, automation, AI, and product thinking to make complex work simpler and more useful.
